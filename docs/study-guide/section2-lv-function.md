2.2 Study Guide Content (Ready to Paste)
A. LV Systolic Function: Definitions & Concepts

LVEF (Left Ventricular Ejection Fraction):
Fraction of LV end-diastolic volume ejected during systole.

Primary Methods:

Biplane Method of Disks (Modified Simpson’s): Gold standard per ASE 2025.

3D Volumetric EF: Preferred when available; eliminates geometric assumptions.

Alternative: Teichholz (only if apical foreshortening limits imaging).

Global Longitudinal Strain (GLS):

Measures myocardial deformation along LV length.

Normal GLS: ≤ –18%

Borderline: –16% to –17%

Abnormal (reduced): > –16% (less negative)

Note: Strain should always be negative for normal LV shortening.

Fractional Shortening (FS):
M-mode index of LV contractility, measured at mid-level short-axis.

Formula: FS% = (LVIDd – LVIDs) / LVIDd × 100

Normal Range: 28–44%

B. LV Diastolic Function: Parameters & Interpretation (ASE 2025)

1. Mitral Inflow (PW Doppler, A4C at leaflet tips):

E wave: Early passive filling.

A wave: Atrial contraction.

E/A Ratio Normal: 1.0 – 2.0

Abnormal: <0.8 (Grade I) or >2.0 (Grade III).

2. Tissue Doppler (TDI e’ velocity, septal & lateral annulus):

Normal Septal e’: ≥ 7 cm/s

Normal Lateral e’: ≥ 10 cm/s

Average E/e’ Ratio:

Normal: < 14

Abnormal: ≥ 14 → suggests elevated LV filling pressure.

3. LA Volume Index (LAVI):

Normal: < 34 mL/m²

Reflects chronic LV diastolic pressure.

4. TR Jet Velocity (optional):

Helps estimate pulmonary pressures; abnormal if > 2.8 m/s.
